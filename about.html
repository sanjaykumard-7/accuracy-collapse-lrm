About This Project

A detailed research and implementation study analyzing how Large Reasoning Models behave when exposed to increasingly difficult reasoning tasks — and why their accuracy collapses.

What is Accuracy Collapse?

Accuracy Collapse refers to a sharp drop in model performance as task complexity increases. Even though models like Flan-T5 can handle simple arithmetic, their reasoning becomes unstable when additional steps or patterns are introduced. This research focuses on measuring this collapse and applying strategies to restore stability.

Understanding how reasoning breaks down across complexity levels

Project Objectives

1️⃣ Analyze Collapse

Study how accuracy drops across levels 1–6 as tasks get harder.

2️⃣ Implement Fixes

Apply mitigation strategies such as decomposition and self-consistency.

3️⃣ Benchmark LRMs

Evaluate small open-source models using real reasoning tasks.

Methodology

Stage 1 – Baseline Evaluation

Test Flan-T5 on arithmetic tasks to measure natural collapse without assistance.

Stage 2 – Visualization

Plot accuracy vs. difficulty and observe trends in reasoning failure.

Stage 3 – Mitigation

Introduce structured improvements such as hints, patterns, and curriculum-style breakdown.

Stage 4 – Task Decomposition

Guide the model step-by-step to improve stability on moderate tasks.

Stage 5 – Self-Consistency Decoding

Run multiple samples and choose the majority answer to boost reliability.
